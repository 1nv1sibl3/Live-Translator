Mumbai

The capital city of Maharashtra, formerly known as Bombay, is derived from the Marathi words "Mumbā" and "Āai," meaning "mother" and "goddess," respectively. The name refers to the local Hindu goddess Mumbadevi.

Pune

The second-largest city is believed to have derived its name from the Sanskrit word "Punya," meaning "virtue." It is often referred to as the "Oxford of the East" due to its educational institutions.

Nagpur

The largest city in central India is named after the Nag River that flows through the city. The name "Nagpur" is believed to be derived from the Sanskrit words "Nag" (snake) and "Pur" (city).
